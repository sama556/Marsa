Farasan Islands

Historic archipelago in the southern Red Sea known for rich marine life, mangroves, and traditional villages.

Island overview

About Farasan Islands

Farasan Islands are a protected archipelago in the Jazan region. They feature coral reefs, pristine beaches, and unique architecture reflecting the area’s trading history.

  • Location: Southern Red Sea, off the coast of Jazan
  • Best season: October – April
  • Ideal for: snorkeling, diving, culture, photography, bird watching
Features

What makes Farasan special

Marine biodiversity

Protected coral reefs home to diverse fish species, dolphins, and sea turtles.

Mangrove forests

Extensive mangrove channels perfect for kayaking and bird watching.

Historic architecture

Traditional stone houses and mosques reflecting centuries of trade history.

Bird sanctuary

Important stopover for migratory birds, especially during spring and autumn.

Restaurants

Dining options on Farasan

Al Bahr Seafood

Fresh catch of the day with simple outdoor seating facing the harbor.

  • Popular dishes: grilled fish, shrimp platters, rice with seafood
  • Best time: sunset dinner after boat trips
  • Local favorite for authentic Red Sea cuisine
Old Village Café

Coffee, tea, and light snacks in a restored traditional building.

  • Great for evening walks and photos
  • Try: Arabic coffee, dates, and local sweets
  • Perfect spot to relax after island tours
